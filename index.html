About Me

02

Where It Started

During the COVID lockdown, I was attending classes online like everyone else. Around the same time, my brother had just started working in graphic design — watching him build things from scratch sparked something in me. He's been the biggest inspiration behind my interest in this field.

Discovering Code

I was initially drawn to design, but quickly realised I didn't always have the visual ideas to match my ambitions. So I took a different approach — I decided to build things through code instead. I started with HTML and CSS, learning how websites are structured and styled, and worked on personal projects to practice what I was learning.

Moving Into Development

Once I was comfortable with the basics, I moved into JavaScript to make things interactive. That led me to join a friend's company as a Frontend Developer Intern — I wanted to see how real-world products are built and how teams actually collaborate. During the internship I also picked up React.js, which changed how I think about building web applications.

A Moment I Still Remember

During my interview, the founder asked: "How would you sell a scrapped phone?" Instead of focusing on its limitations, I said I'd design something on it and list it as a creative piece — on a marketplace or even in a museum. They liked the way I thought about it. That answer still reminds me to look for potential where others see none.

Where I Am Now

After my internship, I shifted focus back to academics. Maths has always been one of my strongest subjects — I genuinely enjoy working with numbers and logic. That's what led me to pursue Chartered Accountancy (CA), with my exams coming up this May.

Today, my interests sit at the intersection of technology and finance — building things on the web while exploring the world of accounting and numbers.
